About Me: My name is Brentley Thomason, and I am a Solutions Architect specializing in full-stack application development, computational graphics, and real-time rendering with OpenGL. Throughout the Computer Science program at Southern New Hampshire University, I developed a versatile technical skill set and a security-conscious approach to software design. This ePortfolio represents the culmination of that growth, showcasing my ability to design, optimize, and secure complex systems through a single, large-scale OpenGL visualization project.

Breadth & Focus: This capstone refined and extended my OpenGL work across three categories: software design & engineering, algorithms & data structures, and databases. Together, these enhancements transformed a tightly coupled rendering system into a modular, high-performance, and persistent 3D application.

Teamwork & Communication: While this project was primarily individual, it reflects skills developed through collaborative experiences in prior courses and my professional role. I have presented architecture diagrams to developers and translated technical decisions for non-technical stakeholders, ensuring shared understanding of goals and trade-offs.

Security Mindset: In the database layer I used parameterized queries and least-privilege access. In the architecture I reduced global state and validated user-controlled parameters, minimizing the surface for unintended behavior.

Success Story: By modularizing the codebase (Renderer, InputManager, SceneManager), adding quadtree spatial partitioning to cut unnecessary draw calls, and integrating a persistent storage layer, I delivered measurable performance gains and real-world utilityâ€”turning a static demo into a reusable, configurable tool.
